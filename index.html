Selected Research

RTA — Reconfigurable Tensor Arrays

UW Pᴺ Computer Engineering Lab · PI Prof. Ang Li · Aug 2025 – Present

Reconfigurable tensor arrays for multiprecision AI and general-purpose computing.

  • Designed a twisted-torus systolic array that eliminates shifter registers and reduces latency vs. conventional systolic arrays.
  • Built a reconfigurable PE supporting general-purpose vector operations beyond matmul, improving utilization across diverse AI workloads.
  • Developed TransDot: a reconfigurable FPU supporting FP4/FP8/FP16/FP32 FMA, subword SIMD, and FP16/FP32 dot-product accumulation.

VISTA — Video Interaction Scoring & Teaching Assistant

SABOL Vision Lab, Cultivate Learning · PI Prof. Gail E. Joseph · Feb 2026 – Present

A multimodal AI system for early-childhood classrooms enabling automated interaction-quality scoring and low-latency teacher coaching.

  • VISTA-QEI: a video analysis pipeline scoring teacher–child interactions with the QEI framework and generating evidence-based coaching reports.
  • VISTA-RT: a real-time coaching system with live video/audio analysis, rolling context memory, and prioritized prompts via wearable interfaces.
  • Implemented working demos and evaluation tools; presented prototypes to interdisciplinary AI and early-learning collaborators.

DICE — CUDA-Compatible CGRA-Based GPGPU

UW Pᴺ Computer Engineering Lab · PI Prof. Ang Li · Oct 2024 – Present

An energy-efficient GPGPU that replaces the SIMD backend with statically scheduled CGRAs.

  • Reduced register-file pressure and control overhead via a CGRA-based execution backend.
  • Built a Temporal Memory Coalescing Unit (TMCU) and swizzled thread-bank mapping to cut register-file bank conflicts.
  • Extended GPGPU-Sim/GPUWattch and built a p-graph compiler for performance/power modeling on GPU-Rodinia.
  • Led an 8-member RTL team to build an RTL implementation and FPGA prototype for architectural validation.
  • 1.90× energy efficiency, 42% lower power, 1.16× performance vs. NVIDIA Turing SMs.

DORA — Python-Based CGRA Prototyping Framework

UW Pᴺ Computer Engineering Lab · PI Prof. Ang Li · Mar 2024 – Present

A highly parameterized CGRA RTL generation framework for rapid architecture exploration.

  • Enabled rapid customization of processing elements, routing networks, and control flows.
  • Accelerated CGRA prototyping through reusable Python-based hardware generation infrastructure.

CPHY — DDR/UCIe-Compatible PHY & LPDDR4x Controller

UW Silicon Systems Research Lab · PI Prof. C.-J. Richard Shi · Mar 2022 – Dec 2023

Memory controller and high-speed PHY taped out in TSMC N28.

  • Led RTL design, mixed-signal integration, and verification of an LPDDR4/4x memory controller and DDR/UCIe-compatible PHY.
  • Performed physical design in TSMC N28, including AMS integration.
  • Integrated the controller and PHY with a RISC-V CPU and a deep-learning accelerator.
